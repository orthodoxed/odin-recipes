Jambalaya

The Ultimate Mish-Mash of Goodness

There are many different types of food that come to mind when presented with the term, "Mish-Mash". Soup, salad, lasagna. Those are arguably delicious when prepared correctly. However, there can be no doubt in our minds that jambalaya is truly one of the most delicious concoctions ever created in the kitchen. I theorize it was likely created initally purely because somebody needed to get rid of the food in their pantry. With it's delcious combination of sausage, shrimp, rice, and delicious cajun herbs and spices, it brings to mind sweet southern banter along with rough and tumble play in the swamps of Lousiana. Some may even be deceived by it's seemingly simple premise: throw a bunch of ingredients together and pray to God it tastes good. However, that couldn't be further from the trough. Though truly a mish mash, one should not underestimate the pure and delicious power of a well crafted plate/bowl of homemade jambalaya and the top notch ingredients that go into it. Jambalaya is truly the epitome of improvisational soul food that stands the test of time.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb of Chicken or Pork
  • 10 oz of Sausage (andoullie, chorizo, or smoked)
  • 1 lb of Seafood (shrimb, crawfish, even crab)
  • 3 tablespoons of cooking oil (olive oil is my preference)
  • 2 tablespoons of Cajun seasoning (premix)
  • 1 diced onion
  • 1 diced green pepper
  • 1 diced red pepper
  • 2 stalks of chopped celery
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 14 oz of canned tomatoes
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp hot pepper sauce
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 3 tsp Worchestershire sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups of white rice (uncooked)
  • 3 cups chicken broth/bone broth
Steps
  1. Heat oil in large pot over medium heat
